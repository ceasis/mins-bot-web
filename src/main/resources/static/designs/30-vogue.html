— Credits —

On the desk, on demand, on its best behavior.

Minsbot is a desktop AI agent — but one with manners. It browses the web on one's behalf, tends to the filing cabinet, drafts replies, and runs an entire library of skills upon one's desktop. The distinction is the address. It lives where you live; it files under your name.

No server sits between you and the work. No pipeline sips from the documents on your disc. No copy of your correspondence is shelved away in the back rooms of a data centre. It is a polite arrangement, and one we have put in writing.

For Spring MMXXVI, the collection introduces four finishes — a browsing primitive, a filesystem primitive, a messaging primitive, and a skills library — each tailored to the user's measurements, each revocable, each unfashionable to share.
