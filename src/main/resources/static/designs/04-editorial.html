Feature · From the Editor

An agent that lives
where the work does.

Mins Bot runs on your own desktop. It browses the web on your behalf, tends to the files you keep, and speaks to the messaging tools you already pay for — all without a middleman. We do not run a server for it. Your files, your drafts, your browsing trails, your credentials: none of it leaves your machine except at your direct instruction, and then only to the provider you picked.

The ambition is modest: to put one general-purpose agent on the side of the person using it, rather than the side of the company renting it. Skills are installable. Integrations are yours to revoke. The audit log is a plain file on your disk. Nothing here is novel; what is uncommon is the refusal to collect.

This edition details the four core capabilities — browsing, filesystem, messaging, skills — and the data policy that governs them all: nothing retained, nothing trained on, nothing sold.

Section IIFour Primitives

The capabilities, dispassionately.

I — Browsing

The agent reads the web like you.

Chromium under your own session. It fills forms, follows links, and writes briefs from actual pages — not a crawler's caricature of them.

II — Filesystem

Only the paths you allow.

An allow-list is the whole policy. Moves, renames, and edits are logged to a local journal you can review and undo.

III — Messaging

In the channels you already keep.

Slack, Discord, Gmail. Tokens live in the OS keychain. Revocation is a single visit to the provider's security page.

IV — Skills

Composable by design.

Skills are manifests plus code. Bring your own, pin to a shared registry, or write one over a coffee. The agent discovers them at launch.

Section IIIOp-Ed · Data Policy

In defense of keeping nothing.

By the editors.

The industry has reached a curious equilibrium in which software performs for its user and reports to its vendor. A desktop agent that inherits this posture would be worse than useless — it would be a burglar with a helpful manner.

Our position is plain: data at rest stays on the device; credentials are handed to the operating system's keychain and never touched otherwise; training pipelines see none of it; model calls travel direct from your hardware to the provider you picked. We do not proxy. We do not log. There is, deliberately, no server on which to mishandle your things.

The full policy is published in plain English. If an incident ever forced us to revise it, we would tell you before we did.
